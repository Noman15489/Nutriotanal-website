Sleeping habits

Establish a consistent sleep schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. This helps regulate your bodyâ€™s internal clock and improves the quality of your sleep. Try to get 7-9 hours of sleep each night, depending on your individual needs.





Be mindful of food and drinks before bed

Avoid Heavy Meals: Eating large or spicy meals close to bedtime can cause discomfort and disrupt sleep. Limit Caffeine and Alcohol: Caffeine can stay in your system for hours, making it harder to fall asleep. Alcohol might make you drowsy initially but can disturb sleep later in the night. Stay Hydrated: Drink enough water throughout the day but try to limit drinks right before bed to avoid waking up in the middle of the night to use the bathroom.






Why is managing sleeping important

Managing sleep is important because it directly impacts your overall health and well-being. Sleep is when your body and mind repair, rejuvenate, and restore energy.
